# Decision: Consolidate Campaign Structure

## Context

Prior to November 2024, the Bella Luna Toys Meta Ads account had accumulated 70+ campaigns over time. Each campaign targeted different audiences, creatives, or objectives, resulting in a fragmented structure where campaigns competed against each other in the same auctions.

## The Problem

- 70+ campaigns running simultaneously, many with overlapping audiences
- Meta's algorithm could not effectively optimize with spend split across so many campaigns
- Auction overlap meant the account was bidding against itself
- No single campaign had enough data volume for meaningful algorithmic learning
- CPA had spiked to $22.25 in October 2024 -- the worst in account history
- ROAS dropped to 6.17x in October 2024 -- the lowest recorded

## The Decision

Consolidate all active campaigns down to 2:

1. **Consolidated Primary Sales -- Adv+ CBO -- 7 Day Click + 1 Day View** -- Primary sales driver using Campaign Budget Optimization
2. **Seasonal Sales -- Adv+ ABO -- 7 Day Click + 1 Day View** -- Seasonal/promotional support using Ad Set Budget Optimization

All other campaigns were paused. The 70+ legacy campaigns remain paused in the account.

## Reasoning

- Consolidation gives the Meta algorithm more data per campaign, improving optimization
- Eliminates self-competition in auctions
- CBO allows Meta to allocate budget to the best-performing ad sets automatically
- Simpler structure is easier to manage and diagnose
- Industry best practice for Advantage+ campaigns favors fewer, larger campaigns

## Expected Outcome

- ROAS improvement from reduced auction overlap and better algorithmic learning
- Lower CPA as the algorithm has more conversion data to optimize against
- Ability to scale spend without fragmenting performance across many small campaigns

## Actual Outcome

The consolidation was decisively validated:

- Nov 2024: 10.99x ROAS at $5,925 spend (up from 6.17x in Oct)
- Dec 2024: 11.00x ROAS at $4,954 spend
- The consolidated structure went on to handle $10k+/month spend in late 2025 and early 2026 while maintaining 11-22x ROAS
- By Q1 2026, the 2-campaign structure was producing 18x+ ROAS at scale

## 4D Score

**Rating: Improved**

This was the single most impactful structural decision in the account's history. Every subsequent scaling decision depended on the clean 2-campaign foundation established here. The move from 70+ fragmented campaigns to 2 consolidated campaigns eliminated self-competition, gave the algorithm the data density it needed, and created a structure that could scale from $2k/month to $10k+/month without breaking. The Sep-Oct 2024 trough (6-8x ROAS) was the low point that motivated the change, and the account never returned to those levels.
