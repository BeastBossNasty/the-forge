# Decision: Maintain Aggressive Spend Into Q1 2026

## Context

After the massive Q4 2025 scale-up ($19,298 combined spend in Nov-Dec at 13.2x ROAS), the typical playbook would be to pull back spend in January-February. The account had done exactly that in early 2025, dropping to $840-$1,221/month in Jan-Feb 2025. However, January 2026 came in at $5,557 spend with 11.80x ROAS and 465 purchases -- significantly stronger than the post-holiday pullback in 2025.

## The Decision

Instead of pulling back to baseline spend levels after the holiday season, maintain aggressive spend at $10,000+/month through Q1 2026. Double down on the momentum rather than retreating to a conservative posture.

## Reasoning

- January 2026 showed no post-holiday efficiency drop: 11.80x ROAS at $5,557 spend
- The algorithm had been continuously learning and optimizing through Q4's high-volume period
- Creative assets were performing well with strong engagement signals
- Pulling back risks losing the learned audience -- the algorithm's optimization resets when starved of data
- The momentum-based strategy says: when it's working, keep pushing
- Year-over-year comparison favored aggression: Jan 2025 had only 93 purchases at $1,221 spend vs Jan 2026's 465 purchases at $5,557 spend

## Expected Outcome

- Sustain 15x+ ROAS through Q1 2026
- Generate significantly more Q1 revenue than any previous Q1
- Prove that the account can sustain high spend year-round, not just during Q4

## Actual Outcome

The decision produced the best results in account history:

- **February 2026:** 21.76x ROAS on $10,055 spend -- the best month ever recorded
  - 1,237 purchases (more than most entire quarters)
  - $218,815 revenue in a single month
  - $8.13 CPA -- among the lowest ever
- **March 2026 (18 days):** 18.18x ROAS on $7,442 spend -- on pace to rival February
  - 891 purchases already, projected ~1,535 for the full month
- **Q1 2026 total (through Mar 18):** $23,055 spend, $419,699 revenue, 2,593 purchases, 18.20x ROAS

For context, all of 2025 generated $310,774 in revenue. Q1 2026 alone has already surpassed that.

## 4D Score

**Rating: Improved**

This was a high-conviction bet that the momentum-based model could work outside of Q4 holiday demand. The results have been extraordinary -- 21.76x ROAS in February is not just good, it rewrites the account's performance ceiling. The decision validated that the consolidated structure, combined with mature algorithmic learning and strong creative, can produce elite results regardless of seasonality. The only watch items are rising CPC ($0.44 to $0.65) and declining CTR (3.89% to 1.91%), which haven't yet impacted ROAS but deserve monitoring.
