# Decision: Aggressively Scale Into Q4 2025 Holiday Season

## Context

By October 2025, the Bella Luna Toys Meta Ads account had been running on the consolidated 2-campaign structure for approximately 12 months. ROAS had recovered from the mid-year softness (7-8x in Apr-Jun) and was consistently holding at 10x+ from July through October (10.71x, 11.00x, 11.80x, 10.20x).

## The Decision

Scale monthly spend from the ~$2,000-$2,500/month range to $9,000-$10,000/month for November and December 2025. This represented a 4-5x increase in spend to capture holiday purchasing demand.

## Reasoning

- ROAS had been 10x+ for 4 consecutive months (Jul-Oct), indicating algorithm stability
- The consolidated campaign structure had proven it could handle varying spend levels efficiently
- Holiday season (Nov-Dec) amplifies purchasing intent for gift-oriented products like Waldorf toys
- Q4 2024 had shown that holiday scaling works: $10,879 combined spend at 11.0x ROAS
- The opportunity cost of not scaling during the highest-intent period was significant
- CTR was trending upward (4.71% Sep, 4.88% Oct), indicating strong creative engagement

## Expected Outcome

- Maintain 10x+ ROAS at 4-5x higher spend levels
- Capture outsized holiday demand before competitors saturate the auction
- Generate significantly more revenue and purchases than Q4 2024

## Actual Outcome

The decision exceeded expectations:

| Metric | Q4 2024 | Q4 2025 | Change |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|
| Spend | $10,879 | $19,298 | +77% |
| Revenue | $119,643 | $254,907 | +113% |
| Purchases | 737 | 1,503 | +104% |
| Blended ROAS | 11.0x | 13.2x | +20% |

- November 2025: 15.13x ROAS at $9,005 spend -- the best high-spend month to that point
- December 2025: 11.53x ROAS at $10,293 spend -- 777 purchases
- CTR hit 5.85-5.88%, the highest sustained engagement in account history
- CPA held at $12.40-$13.25 despite the massive spend increase

## 4D Score

**Rating: Improved**

This decision proved that the consolidated campaign structure could scale aggressively without efficiency loss. The 15.13x ROAS at $9,005 spend in November was the clearest validation -- ROAS actually improved as spend increased, which is the hallmark of a well-optimized campaign structure feeding into strong holiday demand. This success directly set the stage for the Q1 2026 decision to maintain aggressive spend year-round.
