## Key Observations

### Q1 Pullback (Jan-Feb)
- Spend dropped to $840-$1,221/month -- the lowest of the 25-month dataset
- ROAS held at 9x despite minimal spend, indicating solid baseline efficiency
- CPC dropped to $0.17, the lowest recorded -- cheap traffic in the post-holiday lull
- This was a deliberate pullback after Q4 2024 to let the algorithm reset

### Mid-Year Softness (Apr-Jun)
- ROAS dipped to 7.09x-8.27x range
- CPA climbed to $14.50-$19.94, the highest sustained CPA period in the dataset
- June was the weakest month: only 75 purchases at $19.94 CPA
- Summer doldrums for the Waldorf toy category

### Recovery (Jul-Oct)
- July marked the inflection point: ROAS returned to 10.71x on just $943 spend
- August through October showed steady improvement: 11.00x, 11.80x, 10.20x
- CTR climbed from 3.64% to 4.88% -- creative resonance improving
- CPA came back down to the $12-$14 range

### Massive Q4 Scale-Up (Nov-Dec)
- Combined Q4 spend: $19,298 (vs $10,879 in Q4 2024 -- a 77% increase)
- Combined Q4 purchases: 1,503 (vs 737 in Q4 2024 -- a 104% increase)
- Combined Q4 ROAS: ~13.2x (vs ~11.0x in Q4 2024)
- November 2025 was the standout: 15.13x ROAS at $9,005 spend with 726 purchases
- CTR hit 5.85-5.88% -- the highest sustained engagement in account history
- This proved the consolidated campaign structure could scale aggressively

## Notes

The 2025 story is one of patience and payoff. The early-year pullback and mid-year softness were followed by a Q4 that dramatically outperformed the prior year on every metric. The decision to scale aggressively into Q4 (see [[decisions/2025-q4-aggressive-scale]]) was validated by the results. The consolidated 2-campaign structure held up under 4-5x normal spend levels.
