## Key Observations

### Q1-Q2: Gift Season Baseline (Mar-Jun)

- Consistent $38k-$69k monthly spend with 2.35x-2.89x ROAS
- This period covers spring gifting events (Easter, Mother's Day, graduations)
- CPA held in the $43-$44 range -- the account's sweet spot for high-spend months
- April was the most efficient at 2.89x ROAS on the lowest Q1-Q2 spend ($38k)

### July: Best Non-December Month

- ROAS spiked to 3.66x on just $20,288 spend -- the best efficiency outside December
- CPA dropped to $30.65, the lowest of the year
- Spend was pulled back from the $59k/month Q2 levels, and efficiency responded immediately
- This foreshadowed the lesson that would be learned the hard way in August

### Aug-Sep: The Crisis (0.74x and 0.90x ROAS)

- **August:** $19,774 spend generated only $14,660 revenue -- losing $0.26 on every dollar spent
- **September:** $20,736 spend generated only $18,631 revenue -- still underwater at 0.90x
- Combined: $40,510 spent to generate $33,291 in revenue -- a net loss of ~$7,200 in ad spend alone
- CPA exploded to $112-$113, nearly 3x the spring average
- This was a non-gifting dead zone. There is no gift-basket buying occasion in Aug-Sep
- The account maintained $20k/month spend despite no seasonal demand to support it
- See [[decisions/2024-aug-crisis]] for the full analysis

### Oct-Nov: Sluggish Recovery

- October recovered to 2.22x, but CPA remained elevated at $61.96
- November was shockingly weak for a gift basket company: 1.05x ROAS, only 177 purchases
- $28,254 spend in November should have been a holiday ramp-up, but pre-holiday demand was soft
- CPA hit $159.63 in November -- the worst of the entire dataset

### December: The Big Push

- $160,015 in spend -- more than any other month by a factor of 5x
- 1,747 purchases and $304,248 in revenue
- But only 1.90x ROAS -- well below the 4x healthy threshold
- The massive spend overwhelmed the available demand, driving CPA to $91.59
- December accounted for 32% of annual spend and 29% of annual revenue
- The sheer scale of the December push dragged down the annual blended ROAS

## Notes

2024 was a year of extremes. The account oscillated between profitable spring months, a catastrophic summer, an underperforming November, and an overscaled December. The blended 2.11x ROAS means the account barely doubled its ad spend in revenue -- well below the 4x threshold that would indicate healthy performance. The Aug-Sep crisis and the December overscale were the two defining failures. The July pullback (3.66x on $20k) hinted at what a more disciplined approach could look like.
