## Key Observations

### The Massive Pullback

- Annual spend dropped from $496,714 (2024) to $100,840 (2025) -- an 80% reduction
- This was a deliberate strategic pivot after the 2024 losses
- See [[decisions/2025-spend-reduction]] for the full decision log

### Q1 2025: The Worst Quarter in the Dataset

- Jan-Mar combined: $36,574 spend, $26,295 revenue, 283 purchases, 0.72x ROAS
- The account lost approximately $10,279 in ad spend during Q1
- February was the single worst month in the entire 25-month dataset: 0.58x ROAS
- There is virtually no gift-basket demand in January-March outside Valentine's Day
- Even with Valentine's Day in February, the account couldn't break even

### May 2025: Mother's Day Spike

- 7.74x ROAS on just $4,507 spend -- the best single-month efficiency in the dataset
- 360 purchases at $12.52 CPA -- remarkably efficient
- Mother's Day is clearly one of the strongest demand drivers for gift baskets
- Low spend + high seasonal demand = exceptional efficiency

### Jul-Aug 2025: Summer Surprise

- July: 6.77x ROAS on $2,103 spend -- much better than Jul 2024 (3.66x on $20k)
- August: 3.73x ROAS on $7,781 spend -- dramatically better than Aug 2024 (0.74x on $20k)
- The lesson from the 2024 Aug-Sep crisis was applied: keep spend low in non-peak months
- August 2025 proved the off-season can work if spend is controlled

### September-October: Still Weak

- September repeated the 2024 pattern: 0.92x ROAS, losing money
- October improved to 1.19x but still below breakeven after costs
- These months remain dead zones for gift baskets regardless of spend level

### Nov-Dec 2025: Efficient Holiday Season

- November: 6.63x ROAS on $3,762 spend -- dramatically better than Nov 2024 (1.05x on $28k)
- December: 5.62x ROAS on $20,187 spend -- dramatically better than Dec 2024 (1.90x on $160k)
- Combined holiday: $23,949 spend, $138,381 revenue, 783 purchases, 5.78x ROAS
- Dec 2025 spent 87% less than Dec 2024 but achieved nearly 3x better ROAS
- The right-sized holiday spend was the defining success of the year

## Notes

2025 was the year the account learned discipline. The 80% spend reduction was painful in terms of total volume, but it proved that GGB's Meta Ads work best when spend is aligned with seasonal demand peaks. The standout months (May at 7.74x, Jul at 6.77x, Nov at 6.63x, Dec at 5.62x) all shared one trait: spend was right-sized for the available demand. The bottom months (Jan-Mar at 0.58x-0.99x, Sep at 0.92x) shared the opposite trait: spending into dead zones with no gifting occasion. The key question going forward is whether the account can maintain this discipline while gradually scaling holiday-season spend.
