**Category:** Premium gift baskets for holidays and special occasions
**Strategy:** Seasonal demand-aligned spending with off-season maintenance mode

## Account Overview

GourmetGiftBaskets.com is a highly seasonal gift basket business. Meta Ads performance is dominated by gifting occasions -- Mother's Day, Valentine's Day, Christmas, and other holidays. The account's central challenge is extreme volatility: ROAS has ranged from 0.58x (February 2025, losing $0.42 on every dollar) to 7.74x (May 2025, Mother's Day). This makes GGB fundamentally different from steadier accounts like Bella Luna Toys or Bowling.com.

## The Volatility Problem

GGB's month-to-month ROAS swings are extreme and driven almost entirely by the gifting calendar:

| Performance Tier | Months | ROAS Range | Pattern |
|-----------------|--------|------------|---------|
| Strong | May, Jul, Nov-Dec (peak years) | 3.66x -- 7.74x | Gifting occasions with right-sized spend |
| Marginal | Mar, Apr, Jun, Oct | 1.09x -- 2.89x | Minor gifting seasons or transition months |
| Losing Money | Jan, Feb, Mar (off-year), Aug, Sep | 0.58x -- 0.99x | No gifting occasion, spend into a demand vacuum |

This volatility means blended ROAS is a misleading metric. A 2.22x blended average masks months of 7.74x and months of 0.58x. Performance must be evaluated on a seasonal basis.

## The 2024 Crisis and Lesson Learned

In 2024, the account spent $496,714 at 2.11x blended ROAS. Two critical failures defined the year:

1. **Aug-Sep 2024:** Maintained $20k/month spend during a non-gifting dead zone. Result: 0.74x and 0.90x ROAS -- the account lost approximately $7,200 in ad spend. See [[decisions/2024-aug-crisis]].
2. **Dec 2024:** Pushed $160,015 in spend for the holiday season. Generated $304,248 in revenue but only 1.90x ROAS. The massive scale overwhelmed available demand and drove CPA to $91.59.

The lesson: gift basket demand has a hard ceiling in every season. Spend beyond that ceiling produces rapidly diminishing returns.

## The 2025 Efficiency Pivot

In 2025, the account reduced annual spend by 80% (from $497k to $101k) and focused budget on seasonal peaks. See [[decisions/2025-spend-reduction]].

Results:
- Blended ROAS improved from 2.11x to 2.72x (+29%)
- May 2025 (Mother's Day): 7.74x ROAS on $4,507 -- best month in account history
- Dec 2025: 5.62x ROAS on $20,187 -- nearly 3x better efficiency than Dec 2024 (1.90x on $160k)
- Aug 2025: 3.73x on $7,781 -- from losing money (0.74x in 2024) to profitable
- Q1 remained weak (0.58x-0.99x), confirming Jan-Mar is a dead zone for gift baskets

## Open Question: Should the 4x Healthy Threshold Be Seasonal?

The standard 4x ROAS threshold for "healthy" performance may not apply uniformly to GGB. Consider a seasonal framework:

| Season | Proposed Threshold | Reasoning |
|--------|--------------------|-----------|
| Holiday peak (Nov-Dec) | 4x+ | High demand supports strong returns at moderate spend |
| Gifting occasions (Mother's Day, Valentine's, Easter) | 4x+ | Targeted demand spikes support efficiency |
| Transition months (Apr, Jun, Jul, Oct) | 2x+ | Some organic demand, but expect lower returns |
| Dead zone (Jan, Mar, Aug, Sep) | 1x+ (breakeven) or pause entirely | No gifting occasion -- survival mode, not growth mode |

This framework would mean a 2x ROAS in October is acceptable (within threshold) while a 2x ROAS in December signals a problem. The current flat 4x threshold flags almost every month as underperforming, which is not actionable for a business with this much seasonality.
