# Decision: Maintain Spend During Aug-Sep 2024 Dead Zone

## Context

GourmetGiftBaskets.com is a highly seasonal business. Gift basket demand spikes around holidays (Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, Christmas) and drops sharply in between. August and September have no major gifting occasions.

Despite this, the account maintained approximately $20,000/month in Meta Ads spend during August and September 2024.

## The Problem

- **August 2024:** $19,774 spend, $14,660 revenue, 175 purchases, 0.74x ROAS
- **September 2024:** $20,736 spend, $18,631 revenue, 185 purchases, 0.90x ROAS
- Combined: $40,510 spend to generate $33,291 revenue -- a net ad-spend loss of ~$7,200
- CPA exploded to $112-$113, nearly 3x the spring average of $43-$44
- The account was paying $112 to acquire a customer during a period with no natural demand

## What Happened

The spend level was not adjusted for the seasonal demand drop. The same $20k/month budget that worked in Q2 (when Mother's Day and graduation gifting drove 2.38x-2.44x ROAS) was maintained into a period with zero gifting demand. The Meta algorithm could not find enough high-intent gift-basket buyers in August and September, so it served ads to low-quality audiences at high CPAs.

## What Should Have Happened

- Spend should have been reduced to $2,000-$5,000/month in August and September
- July's performance (3.66x ROAS on $20,288) showed improvement when demand existed, but the July 4th gifting window is brief
- A 75-90% spend reduction in Aug-Sep would have saved approximately $30,000-$35,000 in wasted spend

## The Lesson

Gift basket advertising cannot sustain high spend outside of gifting seasons. The demand floor for gift baskets in non-holiday months is very low, and no amount of ad spend can create demand that does not exist. Spend must be aligned to the seasonal demand calendar.

## Impact

This crisis directly informed the 2025 spend reduction strategy (see [[decisions/2025-spend-reduction]]). The 2025 approach of keeping Aug-Sep spend at $3,900-$7,800 produced dramatically better results:

- Aug 2025: 3.73x ROAS on $7,781 (vs. 0.74x on $19,774 in 2024)
- Sep 2025: 0.92x ROAS on $3,932 (vs. 0.90x on $20,736 in 2024) -- still weak, but ~$17,000 less wasted

## 4D Score

**Rating: Worsened**

Maintaining $20k/month spend during a non-gifting dead zone was a clear strategic error. The account lost money for two consecutive months. The signs were visible -- July's efficiency improvement at lower spend levels should have signaled that the account needed a seasonal pullback. Instead, spending continued at Q2 levels into a demand vacuum. This decision (or lack of adjustment) cost the account approximately $7,200 in direct ad-spend losses and inflated the annual CPA.
