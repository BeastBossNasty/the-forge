## Key Observations

### May Spike (8.03x ROAS)
May 2024 was the standout non-Q4 month. 8.03x ROAS on only $980 spend with 62 purchases. This was the highest efficiency month until Nov 2025 broke the record. Worth investigating what creative or audience conditions drove this -- low CPC ($0.74) but also the lowest CTR of the year (2.51%), suggesting the traffic that did click was highly qualified.

### Summer Stability (Jun -- Sep)
The summer months held steady in the 4x-7x ROAS range. Not spectacular but consistently above the healthy threshold. CPA stayed in the $11-$21 range. This is HABA's baseline -- functional but not scaling.

### Q4 Scale-Up (Oct -- Dec)
Budget scaled from ~$900/mo to $3,000+/mo for holiday season. October marked the transition with $1,565 spend and 136 purchases (nearly double September). November was the best month of 2024 at 6.81x ROAS on $3,334 spend with 199 purchases. December held at 6.23x with 183 purchases. The account proved it can scale for Q4 without collapsing efficiency.

### April Dip (3.51x ROAS)
April was the weakest month. 3.51x ROAS, $25.37 CPA, and only 33 purchases. This was early in the account's modern life and likely reflects pre-consolidation campaign structure issues.

## Context

This was the first full year of active management. The account transitioned from legacy Madgicx/1o8 campaign structures toward a consolidated modern approach during Q4. The holiday season performance validated the consolidation thesis but the question remained whether it would sustain into 2025.
