## Key Observations

### Brutal Q1-Q2 (Jan -- Jun)
The first half of 2025 was painful. Two months fell below the healthy floor:
- **February 2025: 2.63x ROAS** -- $36.83 CPA, only 23 purchases. Post-holiday hangover combined with weak creative pipeline.
- **May 2025: 2.23x ROAS** -- The worst month in the entire 25-month dataset. $43.26 CPA, $1.18 CPC, only 21 purchases. CPC nearly doubled the account average.

January through June averaged roughly 3.14x ROAS -- barely above breakeven for most e-commerce margins. CPA was consistently $26-$43 through this stretch. The account was functionally treading water.

### Recovery Started in September (4.60x)
September marked the turn. ROAS climbed to 4.60x, CPA dropped to $21.03, and CPC fell back to $0.57. Not spectacular, but the first month above 4x since January. This set the stage for Q4.

### Q4 Was the Highlight
- **November 2025: 9.72x ROAS** -- Best month ever for HABA. 279 purchases on $2,637 spend. $9.45 CPA. This was a genuine breakout, not just seasonal lift. For context, Nov 2024 was 6.81x -- so YoY improvement was significant.
- **December 2025: 7.61x ROAS** -- 359 purchases (highest volume month ever) on $3,623 spend. $10.09 CPA. Strong follow-through.

Q4 2025 alone: ~$7,514 spend, ~$58,404 revenue, ~702 purchases, ~7.77x blended ROAS.

### Still Dramatically Underperforming Bella Luna
Despite the Q4 highs, HABA's 6.13x annual blended ROAS is less than half of Bella Luna's 13.4x on similar products in the same category (wooden toys). The gap is structural:
- HABA's CPC runs 2-3x higher than Bella Luna
- Less UGC-friendly creative (German brand positioning vs. whimsical Bella Luna aesthetic)
- American market positioning remains unclear
- Outside of Q4, HABA struggles to maintain even 4x ROAS consistently

### CPC Problem
The CPC trend tells a story. In 2024, CPC averaged around $0.54. In 2025 Q1-Q2, it spiked to $0.66-$1.27. Even in the strong Q4, CPC was $0.56-$0.65. HABA pays more per click than Bella Luna does, and the traffic converts at lower rates. This is a creative and positioning problem, not a media buying problem.
