# Decision: Account Restructure -- Consolidation from Legacy Campaigns

## Context

HABA's Meta ad account had accumulated 40+ campaigns from prior management eras (Madgicx automated campaigns, 1o8 campaign structures, various test campaigns). Budget was fragmented across too many campaigns, none of which had sufficient spend to exit learning phase consistently.

This followed Bella Luna's successful consolidation, which produced dramatic ROAS improvement by funneling budget into a single Advantage+ shopping campaign supplemented by targeted ABO campaigns for seasonal pushes.

## Decision

Consolidate HABA's account into a simplified structure:
1. **Consolidated Adv+ Sales Campaign (Evergreen)** -- Primary always-on campaign at $60/day
2. **Seasonal Promotions ABO** -- Activated for time-sensitive offers
3. Pause all legacy campaigns (40+ campaigns from Madgicx/1o8 eras)

## Rationale

- Bella Luna's consolidation produced a jump from ~5x to 13.4x blended ROAS
- Fragmented budget was preventing any single campaign from optimizing properly
- Modern Advantage+ campaigns benefit from consolidation (broader signal, faster learning)
- Simplifies management and reduces noise in reporting

## Outcome

### What Worked
- Q4 2024 scaled effectively: Nov hit 6.81x on $3,334 spend, Dec held at 6.23x on $3,103
- CPA compressed during Q4 to $11-17 range (down from $18-25 earlier in 2024)
- Account became manageable -- clear signal on what was and was not working

### What Did Not Work
- Results did not sustain into 2025 the way Bella Luna's did
- Q1-Q2 2025 was brutal (2.23x-3.15x ROAS for five consecutive months)
- The consolidation did not solve HABA's structural creative and positioning problems
- Bella Luna's consolidation produced a permanent step-change; HABA's produced a seasonal bump

## 4D Score: Neutral

ROAS improved during Q4 2024 but did not sustain. The consolidation was necessary and correct from a structural standpoint -- the old campaign fragmentation was clearly suboptimal. But the gains were temporary and tied to seasonal demand rather than a fundamental efficiency improvement. The account still oscillates between 3x and 4x outside of holiday periods.

## Review Notes

The core issue is not campaign structure. HABA's problems are upstream:
- Creative assets that do not resonate with American audiences
- German brand positioning that creates friction in the US market
- Higher CPCs than Bella Luna despite similar products
- The consolidation removed structural drag but could not fix the demand-side problem
