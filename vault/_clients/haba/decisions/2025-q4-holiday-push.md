# Decision: Q4 2025 Holiday Budget Push

## Context

HABA had just come through a difficult Q1-Q2 2025 (five months in the 2.23x-3.21x ROAS range). September 2025 showed the first signs of recovery at 4.60x ROAS. With Q4 approaching and the 2024 holiday season as a positive precedent (6.81x Nov, 6.23x Dec), the decision was made to scale budget aggressively for the holiday window.

## Decision

Scale HABA's Meta ad budget for Q4 2025 holidays, increasing spend through the Consolidated Evergreen campaign and activating Seasonal Promotions ABO for holiday-specific offers. Let the Advantage+ algorithm capture holiday demand.

## Budget Trajectory

| Month | Spend | Context |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Sep 2025 | $736 | Baseline recovery month |
| Oct 2025 | $1,254 | Ramp-up begins |
| Nov 2025 | $2,637 | Full holiday push |
| Dec 2025 | $3,623 | Peak holiday spend |
| Jan 2026 | $2,183 | Post-holiday wind-down |

## Outcome

### The Win
- **November 2025: 9.72x ROAS** -- Best month in HABA's entire history. 279 purchases at $9.45 CPA on $2,637 spend. This beat Nov 2024 (6.81x) by 43%.
- **December 2025: 7.61x ROAS** -- 359 purchases (highest volume month ever) at $10.09 CPA on $3,623 spend. Also beat Dec 2024 (6.23x) by 22%.
- Q4 2025 total: ~$7,514 spend, ~$58,404 revenue, ~702 purchases, ~7.77x blended ROAS.

### The Caveat
- **January 2026 dropped to 3.18x ROAS.** The post-holiday regression happened again, just like Jan 2025 (4.19x). The holiday performance did not carry forward.
- The account returned to its baseline 3-4x ROAS pattern within 30 days of the holiday window closing.
- This confirms HABA's Q4 performance is demand-driven (holiday gift buying for wooden toys) rather than a structural improvement in the ad account.

## 4D Score: Improved

The decision to scale for Q4 was correct and produced HABA's two best months ever. YoY improvement was significant (Nov up 43%, Dec up 22%). Budget allocation was well-timed and the Advantage+ campaign handled the scale without efficiency collapse.

However, the "improved" score comes with the caveat that this is seasonal, not structural. HABA continues to be a holiday-dependent account. The Q4 push strategy should be repeated annually, but it does not solve the 9-month problem of mediocre off-season performance.

## Implications

- Budget Q4 2026 aggressively -- the pattern is now validated across two consecutive years
- Do not expect holiday momentum to carry into Q1 -- plan for 3-4x ROAS in January
- The real opportunity is finding a way to lift the off-season floor from 3x to 5x+
- Creative and positioning work (not media buying) is the lever for off-season improvement
